Grief Support

There is no “just a pet” here.

Pets are woven into ordinary life in extraordinary ways. They witness routines, recovery, joy, loneliness, hard seasons, and quiet nights. When they are gone, the house can feel different in a way words struggle to explain.

Guilt, numbness, anger, relief after a long illness, and sadness can all exist together. None of those feelings make the love less real.

When grief is loud, make the next decision smaller:

  • Breathe: pause for three slow breaths.
  • Witness: say, “They were loved, and I am trying to honor them.”
  • Act gently: ask one question or make one call.

What should I do if my pet passed at home?

Can I call before my pet passes?

Yes. Planning ahead can reduce stress and help you understand the options before the hardest moment arrives.

Do I need to know exactly what I want?
